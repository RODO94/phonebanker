# Safeguarding

Phonebankers will hear about evictions in progress, homelessness, domestic abuse, and other situations that are distressing to take in. This doc covers how the service — including the app — supports volunteers and members through those moments.

LRU may already have written safeguarding guidelines that should sit above this doc. **Action: organiser to confirm with union staff what exists.** Until then, this is the working position.

---

## Principles

1. **Active listening first.** The volunteer's job on a hard call is to hear the person, not to solve their situation. The script should reinforce this; the app should not nudge volunteers to rush.
2. **Judgement over rules.** Volunteers decide when a call is beyond them. The service supports that judgement by making it easy to step back, ask for help, and debrief.
3. **No volunteer phonebanks alone.** Even on a remote-only night, the Zoom room is open and the organiser is reachable.
4. **The organiser checks in.** After any session with hard calls, the organiser is responsible for a 1:1 check-in — that day or the next.

---

## In the moment — what a volunteer can do

| Situation | Recommended action |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Member in immediate distress (homelessness, abuse, crisis) | Stay on the call, listen, take their situation seriously. Don't promise outcomes. Note in the log that follow-up is needed. |
| Volunteer feels out of their depth mid-call | Tell the member you'll get someone with more experience to call back. End the call kindly. Flag to the organiser. |
| Volunteer needs to stop | Step away from the app — their assigned contact will be released after 30 min. No need to "hand off" a call to the system. |
| Member is abusive to the volunteer | End the call. Tell the organiser immediately. Skip the contact and add a note in the next-day Airtable review. |

The script for the night should include a short "if you hear X, here's what to say" section written by the organiser.

---

## Routes to help during a session

- **Zoom chat** — primary route for most volunteers
- **WhatsApp group** — for those not on Zoom
- **In person** — when running a hybrid or physical session
- **The organiser is also phonebanking** — they can pause to help; design the session capacity around this

---

## After the session

- **Community debrief**: open space at the end of the session for whoever wants to stay. Hard calls discussed here.
- **1:1 check-in**: organiser's responsibility for any volunteer who took a difficult call, including those who left early.
- **Member follow-up next day**: organiser checks in with the member where appropriate.
- **Onward routing**: complex issues go to staff caseworkers or to the next weekly solidarity session.

---

## What the app does NOT do

- Does not offer counselling-style prompts or scripted responses to crisis
- Does not surface helpline numbers (these belong in the script and the union's own materials, not hardcoded)
- Does not auto-flag calls as "distressing" — that's the volunteer's judgement and the organiser's review
